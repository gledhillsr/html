BRIGHTON VOLUNTEER SKI PATROL INFORMATION SHEET



The Brighton Ski Patrol is composed of two groups of patrollers, Professionals and Volunteers. The Professional Patrollers are full time employees of the Brighton Ski Resort. They report directly to the Pro Patrol Director, Patrick Eibs. The Volunteer Patrollers work on a part-time basis on weekends and nights. The volunteer patrollers report directly the Volunteer Patrol Director, Wolfgang Schwurack, and indirectly to the Pro Patrol Director.



Brighton's Volunteer Ski Patrol's training program consists of Candidate and Probationary training. You must complete both training programs, Candidate and Probationary training, to become a member of the Brighton Volunteer Ski Patrol and of the National Ski Patrol System (NSPS).



During your Candidate training, you must successfully complete Cardio Pulmonary Rescucitation (CPR), Outdoor Emergency Care (OEC), and on-the-hill training. Once the Candidate training has been successfully completed, you will be eligible to become a Probationary Patroller. After completing a 10-day probationary period, you will be eligible to become a full member of the Patrol. A general schedule and details of your training are given below:

Candidate Training

Candidates must complete the following training, which is provided to you by the Brighton Patrol:



· Successfully pass the Preliminary Ski/Snowboard Test held in the spring. Those passing this test have priority in filling slots in the Outdoor Emergency Care course. All Candidates must pass this test to participate in the On-the-hill training.


Note: Candidates who have not taken the Preliminary Ski/Snowboard test may still take the OEC course if space is available. However, they will have to pass the test when the resort opens and conditions allow, to continue the training program.

· Successfully complete the NSPS Outdoor Emergency course. This is a nationally-administered, EMT-level, emergency care course established by the NSPS specifically for ski patrollers. This course covers approximately 90 hours of course work.

· Successfully complete either the American Red Cross Professional Rescuer or American Heart Association CPR class.



· Successfully complete the Brighton Patrol's On-the-Hill Ski, Snowboard, and Toboggan Candidate course. This course is taught once each year. The course work will start when the resort opens and run through the first part of February. A final performance-based test of Skiing, Snowboarding, and Toboggan-Handling skills will conclude the course. Candidates must pass this performance test to become Probationary Patrollers. Each candidate must plan on devoting at least one weekend day every week for the term of the course.
