January 27, 2009 

The Brighton Volunteer Ski Patrol requests your support of our 2009 Patrol Day fund raiser by donating a prize that will be given away in a raffle or auction to take place at Brighton Resort on Saturday, the 14th of March. 

In addition to the raffle and auction, other Patrol Day activities include:

  • Avalanche Dog Demonstration
  • Ren Diner offering Sloppy Joes at the base of the Crest lift.
  • Bake Goods for Sale
 

Each year, we select a worthy non-profit organization to benefit from Patrol Day through donations and publicity.  This season a portion of the proceeds will be donated to the Prostrate Cancer Foundation (http://www.prostatecancerfoundation.org).  One reason for our selection is that one of our Patrollers was diagnosed with prostate cancer and is struggling to fight this form of cancer.  

The Brighton Volunteer Ski Patrol is dedicated to promote a safer recreational environment through education and safety awareness on the slopes.  As first responders, we provide free emergency medical care and transportation to injured guests, frequently in extreme weather conditions.  In addition, we have provided educational services to the community such as CPR classes. 

Each patroller volunteers more than 190 hours a year, which equates to over 19,500 volunteer hours for the entire patrol.  In addition, each patroller provides their equipment and uniforms.  

The remaining funds will be used for a variety of purposes, including medical and rescue equipment, medical and mountain skills training equipment, support of educational programs, as well as maintenance and upgrades of patrol facilities.
