Proposal:  Funding and Patrol Funding 

Submitted by:  Eileen Barlage  

                  March, 2008 

Approved:  April 2, 2008 

        Option: Patrollers can assist the patrol with raising funds, and also have an option to not pay dues. 

        Proposal: 

        1. Fundraiser needs to be approved by directorship.  The directorship will determine if area approval is required and will help obtain such approval if necessary.
        2. Two or more patrollers will handle money, which will be deposited in patrol funds.  There will be a paper accounting submitted to the patrol with the funds.
        3. Only people who participate in the fundraiser will get credit.
        4. Patroller must raise $150 (approximately double the dues) to have dues covered.  If $100 is raised, but less than $150, Â½ of the dues will be covered.  Nothing less than $100 will count toward dues.
        5. If we have a large fundraiser, such as Ski Patrol Day or Ski Swap, this option may not apply at the discretion of the directorship.
        6. If combined with other organizations, such as MS and Ski Patrol, the patroller will be credited only the amount donated to the Brighton patrol.
 

        History/Rationale: 

        1. Several patrollers are good at raising funds for charity, and do so on a regular basis.
        2. We need operating funds, and other funds for special expenses.
        3. Some patrollers would rather raise funds than pay dues.
