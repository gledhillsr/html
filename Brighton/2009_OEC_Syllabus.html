Brighton Ski Patrol

Outdoor Emergency Care 2009

Syllabus 

Location: New York Life Offices, 150 West Civic Center Drive, Suite 600, Sandy.  Building has Cadence on outside.  Park in garage and enter through front door facing street.  Class will be from 6:30-9:30PM

 
Expectations: Students are to read and complete the workbook for chapters before class.  Workbooks are turned in at the beginning of class to check that chapters have been completed.  Quizzes may be given any time.  Most nights more than half the class time will be hands practicing skill and becoming familiar with the equipment.  

Attendance: Students are expected to attend each class.  Missing more than two classes will require instructor approval.  This class covers a lot of material and missing just one class could make it difficult to catch up. 

Materials: Outdoor Emergency Care: Comprehensive Prehospital Care for Non-urban Settings,

            Fourth Edition.

            Outdoor Emergency Care: Workbook.

            Blood Pressure cuff and stethoscope

            Watch with second hand 

Resources: Brighton Ski Patrol website: www.brightonnsp.org

            OEC zone website: www.oeczone.com 

Evaluating Criteria: Written Midterm, Practical Midterm, Written Final and Practical Final, attendance, workbook completion, class participation and team interaction.

Class is finally over.   You have completed the NSPS Outdoor Emergency Care class.  Brighton has extra training to prepare you to be proficient in your OEC training on our hill.  Now for some fun!  The dates are pending for when the area opens – earlier or later.    For the on the hill scenarios you will be working in teams where you will practice leading and assisting at accident sites.  These scenarios will simulate actual accidents and allow you to experience how to handle an accident from beginning to end on the hill.
